Market gap

The missing piece is guidance at the moment of doubt.

Every existing tool solves one piece. FakeOut combines signals and tells users what to do next.

Fact-checkers (Snopes, PolitiFact)

Useful for public claims, weaker for private forwards, screenshots, and real-time messages.

Scam blockers (Guardio, Norton Safe Web)

Good for known spam and malicious URLs, weaker for ambiguous links and social messages.

Deepfake tools (Reality Defender, Hive)

Often narrow, technically demanding, or priced for enterprise buyers, not everyday users.

FakeOut opening

One workflow for content, context, risk, and next action, designed for the person who just got a suspicious WhatsApp forward.

08 / 11
Risks

A trust product has to be careful by design.

FakeOut reduces risk without pretending any system can be perfectly certain.

Accuracy

Risk bands and confidence levels prevent overconfidence. Outputs show evidence, not just verdicts. The product never claims binary true/false.

Privacy + GDPR

Private by default. User content is not stored or used for training. GDPR-compliant design from day one. Opt-in only for saved examples.

Cost

Text and link checks stay cheap. Expensive media checks are gated behind paid tiers to keep margins viable.

Competition

Win on workflow, local language support, plain-language explanation, and actionable next steps that enterprise tools skip.
